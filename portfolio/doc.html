Project Brief

Doc.ai is a conversational platform for on-demand, quantified biology.

We are entering a world where artificial intelligence and natural language understanding are bringing unmatched benefits to healthcare. Thus, the platforms advanced natural language dialog system is designed for vertical medical domains, and can generate insights on blood, genomics, micrbiome, environmental and anatomical data

This project is a design challenge I personally undertook when applying to Doc.ai growing team so I may stand out amongst the pool of candidates being considered

Concept

The platform is at its core a chat-bot, thus it was imperative I approach the design having conversational platform guidelines in mind

I decided the application must revolve around the chat bot but at the same time make the other aspects of the application equally engaging. The pages I decided were: Home, Medical Records, Upload Documents, Chatbot, Settings and Profile

Identity

The logo was already designed by the company. I used their .png file after asking permission from the team in San Fransisco.

Colors

Although red is not the preferred colour of choice for medical professionals, I felt it will enhance the user experience when red is constrasted with white while navigating the application.

Dashboard

The home screen has three major sections:

Appointments The app shows any new doctor appointments that are added via the doctor affiliate program

Recent Activity In this section users can glance at the following types of notifications: chatbot asking to connect, document uploads and medical news

My Data This section allows the user to upload documents or jump to the documents page with one click. The reason these two functions are prominently placed in the home screen is due to the fact that the app revolves around learning information from your medical documents thus it should not be a burden for the user to upload and engage with existing documents

Upload Documents

As the platforms core value to users is the cognitive capability to read and understand medical reports, users have to find uploading an easy and pleasant task. Hence this is the reason why the interface is kept simple with a progress bar and a completed list underneath. This structure gives the user the freedom to delete any uploaded documents without having to go into the medical history tab.

Records Manager

It will not be unusual to assume that there will be numerous short documents uploaded into the platform for constant diagnosis from the bot. To facilitate this, the manager helps sort the documents from most relevant to least while easily allowing the user to navigate with the infinite scroll. The little bar in the bottom gives the user a snapshot idea of how regular they have been with their document uploads.

Chat-bot

All of the apps functions revolve around this screen. Doc.ai can advice not only about current illness but also about future illnesses that you might be prone to. The chat interface was designed with natural language processing in mind. It needs to be set up in a manner that engages the user and makes them believe they might just conversing with a real person
